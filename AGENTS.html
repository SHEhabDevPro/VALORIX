Class Roles

Duelist

Role Definition: Offensive powerhouse responsible for leading engagements and securing eliminations.

Key Responsibilities: Initiate Engagements: Lead attacks and create opportunities. Aim for High Impact: Secure kills and excel in 1v1 or 1vX situations. Use Abilities Aggressively: Utilize abilities to enhance combat effectiveness, such as dashes, flashes, or smokes. Map Control and Entry Frags: Take control of key areas and push through chokepoints. Positioning and Awareness: Maintain good positioning and stay aware of surroundings. Communicate with Team: Share information and coordinate with teammates.

Essential Skills: Sharp Reflexes and Accuracy: Key for quick reactions and precise aim. Understanding of Abilities: Master the use of your unique abilities. Map Knowledge: Familiarize yourself with map layouts and common engagement points. Adaptability: Adjust playstyle based on situations and opponents. Confidence and Aggression: Embrace a bold playstyle and take calculated risks. Team Coordination: Align aggressive plays with team strategy.

Initiator

Role Definition: Tactical support designed to create opportunities and disrupt enemy defenses, paving the way for teammates.

Key Responsibilities: Disrupt Enemy Setup: Use abilities to unsettle enemy positions and force mistakes. Create Opportunities: Enable team strategies by providing openings and advantages. Support Team Entry: Facilitate safe entry into contested areas by revealing or disabling enemies. Provide Utility: Utilize gadgets to provide vision, crowd control, or debuffs. Coordinate with Teammates: Work closely with teammates to maximize impact of abilities and strategies.

Essential Skills: Strategic Thinking: Plan and execute abilities to maximize their impact. Map Awareness: Understand common defensive setups and key engagement areas. Communication: Share information and coordinate with teammates effectively. Timing: Use abilities at the right moment to ensure they have the maximum impact. Adaptability: Adjust tactics based on the flow of the game and enemy actions. Team Support: Provide consistent support and enable team plays.

Sentinel

Role Definition: Defensive specialist focused on protecting teammates, holding critical areas, and providing utility for team defense.

Key Responsibilities: Secure Key Areas: Establish and maintain control over important locations on the map. Protect Teammates: Provide defensive abilities that shield or heal allies. Monitor and Respond: Keep an eye on potential threats and react to enemy movements. Place Defensive Utility: Set up traps or other utilities to deter or damage enemies. Communication: Relay important information about enemy positions and movements.

Essential Skills: Defensive Strategy: Design and execute defensive plans effectively. Map Knowledge: Understand where to place utilities for maximum effect. Awareness: Stay alert to enemy movements and changes in the game flow. Team Support: Actively contribute to the team's defensive efforts. Precision: Ensure defensive utilities are placed accurately and effectively. Communication: Share critical information with the team to coordinate defense.

See Agents

Controller

Role Definition: Versatile specialist adept at controlling the battlefield, often through area denial or crowd control.

Key Responsibilities: Control Areas: Use abilities to block or influence areas of the map, making it difficult for enemies to advance. Deny Access: Utilize abilities to limit enemy movement or vision. Create Safe Passage: Clear or create safe paths for teammates to advance or retreat. Support Team Strategy: Provide utility that complements team plans and strategies. Coordinate and Adapt: Adjust abilities and tactics based on the flow of the game and enemy actions.

Essential Skills: Area Control: Master the use of abilities to control or deny key areas. Strategic Placement: Position abilities to maximize their effectiveness. Adaptability: Adjust tactics based on the state of the game and enemy behavior. Communication: Work with teammates to ensure abilities align with team strategies. Timing: Deploy abilities at the optimal moment to disrupt enemy plans. Map Knowledge: Understand how to use abilities effectively across different map areas.
